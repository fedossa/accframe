This is an experiment on decision-making. You have been randomly and anonymously paired with another participant. One of you will be selected at random to be participant A; the other will be participant B. You will learn whether you are participant A or B prior to making any decision.

{{ if C.NUM_ROUNDS == 1 }} This experiment will be played for only 1 round. First, {{ else }} This experiment will be played over {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds. Throughout the experiment, you will remain in the same role. Each round, {{ endif }} participant A has to choose an amount that she or he will send to participant B. This amount can be any integer between 0 and {{ C.WAGE_MAX }}. Next, participant B has to decide on a payment. She or he can choose between 10 different payments ranging from 0 to 18. Each payment is linked to a multiplier between 0.1 and 1 in the way that a higher payment implies a higher multiple. The link is as follows:

{{ include_sibling 'multiplier_table.html' }}

The payoff to participant B will be the amount sent by participant A minus the chosen payment. The payoff of participant A will be based on the difference of 100 minus the amount sent to participant B. This difference will be multiplied by the multiplier that corresponds to the payment that participant B has chosen to determine the payoff. If this value is not an integer it will be rounded to yield the payoff.

So, for example, if participant A chooses to send an amount 50 to participant B and participant B chooses to pay 10, the payoff to participant B will be 50 - 10 = 40 and the payoff to participant A will be (100 - 50) * 0.7 = 35.

{{ if C.NUM_ROUNDS == 1 }} The objective of the game is to maximize your payoff. {{ else }} The payoffs that both players receive in each round increase their private wealth. The objective of the game is to maximize your private wealth over the duration of the game. {{ endif }}
