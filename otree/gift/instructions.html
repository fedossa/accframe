This is an experiment on decision-making. You have been randomly and anonymously paired with another participant. In the experiment, there are two roles: Participant A and Participant B. The roles are randomly assigned to you and the other participant. You will learn whether your role is Participant A or Participant B prior to making any decision.

In the experiment, you and the other participant can earn points. Points will be exchanged for money at the end of the experiment.

{{ if C.NUM_ROUNDS > 1 }} This experiment consists of {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds. Throughout the experiment, you will remain in the same role. The points that you earn in each round are added up. A round proceeds as follows: {{ endif }} Participant A starts with an endowment of 100 points. Participant B starts with an endowment of 0 points. First, Participant A decides how many points they want to transfer to Participant B. The amount transferred can be any integer between 0 and {{ C.WAGE_MAX }}. Next, Participant B selects a multiplier from the table below. The multiplier selected by Participant B determines the payoff of Participant A. Specifically, the payoff of Participant A is calculated as follows:

(100 - transfer to Participant B) * multiplier.

Participant B can select multipliers between 0.1 and 1.0. Higher multipliers are more costly for Participant B.

In summary, the payoffs are calculated as follows:

  • Payoff Participant A = (100 - transfer to Participant B) * multiplier
  • Payoff Participant B = Transfer received from Participant A - cost of multiplier

Example:

  • Suppose that Participant A chooses to transfer 40 points to Participant B and Participant B then chooses the multiplier 0.7, which costs 10 points. The payoff of Participant A then is (100 points - 40 points) * 0.7 = 42 points. The payoff of Participant B then is 40 points - 10 points = 30 points.
