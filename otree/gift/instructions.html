This is an experiment on decision-making. Your objective in this experiment is maximizing your points. You have been randomly and anonymously paired with another participant. In the experiment, there are two roles: participant A and participant B. The roles are randomly assigned to you and the other participant. You will learn whether your role is participant A or participant B prior to making any decision.

{{ if C.NUM_ROUNDS == 1 }} This experiment will be played for only 1 round. Participant A {{ else }} This experiment will be played over {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds. The points that you receive in each round are accumulated. Throughout the experiment, you will remain in the same role. Each round, participant A {{ endif }} starts with an endowment of 100 points and participant B starts with an endowment of 0 points. First, participant A decides how many points they want to transfer to participant B. The amount transferred can be any integer between 0 and {{ C.WAGE_MAX }}. Next, participant B selects a multiplier from the table below. The multiplier selected by participant B determines the payoff of participant A. Specifically, the payoff of participant A is calculated as follows:

(100 - transfer to participant B) * multiplier.

Participant B can select multipliers between 0.1 and 1.0. Higher multipliers are more costly for participant B.

In summary, the payoffs are calculated as follows:

  • Payoff participant A = (100 - transfer to participant B) * multiplier
  • Payoff participant B = Transfer received from participant A - cost of multiplier

Example:

  • Suppose that participant A chooses to transfer 30 points to participant B and participant B then chooses the multiplier 0.7, which costs 10 points. The payoff of participant A then is (100 points - 30 points) * 0.7 = 49 points. The payoff of participant B then is 30 points - 10 points = 20 points.
