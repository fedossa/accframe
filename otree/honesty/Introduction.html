This is an experiment on decision-making. In the experiment, there are two roles: Participant A and Participant B. You will act as Participant A, the computer of the organizers will act as Participant B Your task as Participant A is to report a number of points to Participant B. You will be privately informed about this number before you will be asked to report it. Participant B will not be informed about the number. Both you and Participant B know the probability distribution of the number that you need to report. This probability distribution is a uniform distribution with a minimum of 4,000 points, a maximum of 6,000 points, and possible increments of 50 points. Participant B will start with a total of 6,000 points. You can report any number that is between the actual number and 6,000 (the maximum number), including these values. Participant B will always send you the number of points that you report, independent of what the actual number is. The difference between the reported number and the actual number is yours to keep. You will also receive an additional fixed amount of 250 points. Thus, your payoff will be determined as follows: 250 + (reported number - actual number).

Here are two examples.

  • Example 1: Suppose you observe that the actual number is 5,000 and you also report that the number is 5,000. Then your payoff is 250 + (5,000 - 5,000) = 250.
  • Example 2: Suppose you observe that the actual number is 4,500 and you report that the number is 5,500. Then your payoff is 250 + (5,500 - 4,500) = 1,250.
