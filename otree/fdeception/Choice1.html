This is a short experiment on corporate behavior. In this experiment, you will represent the role of a corporate accountant that has to inform corporate headquarters about the desirability of two alternative investment opportunities.

Corporate headquarters is represented by another participant. There are two alternative investments A and B. Only you (the accountant) can observe their actual profitability.

Both investments create private payoff to you and to corporate headquarters These are the payoffs of the two investment options:

  • Investment A: ${{ C.AMOUNT_A1 }} to you and ${{ C.AMOUNT_A2 }} to corporate headquarters.
  • Investment B: ${{ C.AMOUNT_B1 }} to you and ${{ C.AMOUNT_B2 }} to corporate headquarters.

In the end, corporate headquarters will decide which investment to make and this decision will determine the payoffs. But they will make this decision solely informed the accounting report that you send about the two investment options.

Which accounting report do you want to send to corporate headquarters?

  • Accounting Report 1: “Investment A will be more profitable to you then investment B.”
  • Accounting Report 2: “Investment B will be more profitable to you then investment A.”

Corporate headquarters will receive your accounting report, and based on this to choose either investment A or B. To repeat, this investment decision will determine the payments in the experiment. However, corporate headquarters will never learn the actual payoffs of the investment that they did not pick (that is, they will never know whether your accounting report was true or not). Moreover, they will never know the payoffs that you received according to the different options.

In this experiment, we will pay the you and the participant representing corporate headquarters according to the choice made by corporate headquarters. The payment will be made in private.
