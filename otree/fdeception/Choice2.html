This is a short experiment on corporate behavior. In this experiment, you will represent the role of corporate headquarters that has to decide between to alternative investment options A and B. You are informed about the relative favorability of these two investment options by receiving a report from an accountant. inform corporate headquarters about the desirability of two alternative investment opportunities.

This accountant is represented by another experimental participant. As an accountant, he is able to observe the payoffs of the two investment options. Both options generate a payoff to you (corporate headquarters) and the other participant (the accountant).

Based on his or her private information, the accountant will send you an accounting report that advises you on which investment to take. The payments to both participants then depend on the investment chosen by you.

The accounting report could take one of the following two forms:

  • Accounting Report 1: “Investment A will be more profitable to you then investment B.”
  • Accounting Report 2: “Investment B will be more profitable to you then investment A.”

Your accountant decided to send you Report {{message}}.

We now ask you to choose either investment A or B. Your choice will determine the payments in the experiment. You will never know the payoffs of the other investment (that is, if the accounting report sent by your accountant was true or not). Moreover, you will never know the payoffs your accountant could be paid with the other investment. We will pay the two of you according to the investment you pick.
